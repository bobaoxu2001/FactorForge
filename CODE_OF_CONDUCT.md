# Code of Conduct

## Our standards

FactorForge aims to be a constructive, technical, and welcoming project space. Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and patient in issues, discussions, reviews, and commits.
- Critique code, assumptions, tests, and documentation rather than people.
- Keep feedback specific and actionable.
- Be honest about uncertainty, limitations, and tradeoffs.
- Avoid harassment, insults, discriminatory language, or personal attacks.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project spaces including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, and other maintainer-run channels for FactorForge.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues or pull requests; or block participation when behavior is disruptive, abusive, or unsafe for the project community.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to the project maintainer through GitHub. Include links, screenshots, or timestamps when useful, but do not share private personal information beyond what is necessary.
